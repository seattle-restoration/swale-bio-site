Why the U-District Needs Bioswales

The University District sits at the southern edge of the Ravenna Creek drainage area, where the creek historically emptied into Union Bay. It's one of the densest neighborhoods in the watershed - meaning more impervious surface, more runoff, and more pressure on the storm system.

For property owners in the U-District, a bioswale is both a practical investment and a statement. Dense neighborhoods generate the most stormwater per acre, which means each bioswale here has outsized impact. Whether you own a single-family home or a small multifamily building, green infrastructure reduces your stormwater burden and adds value.

The U-District's mix of housing types - from craftsman homes to small apartment buildings - means bioswale designs need to be flexible. We customize every installation for the lot size, soil conditions, and building type, maximizing stormwater capture even on compact urban properties.
